# V4-H1 Exact 120-Minute Ranker

Status: completed development experiment; B0 and R1 rejected. No shadow data was opened and no artifact is serving-eligible.

## Hypothesis

The C8 failure audit showed that relative performance improved at longer horizons. V4-H1 therefore changed the primary ranking horizon and decision cadence to 120 minutes while retaining the point-in-time S&P 500 universe, technical feature schema, 10 bps round-trip cost, development cutoff, top-10 selection, and B0/R1 families.

The minimum fold row check was scaled from 100,000 to 50,000 because the decision cadence halved the number of correlated rows. The substantive 60-session training window, four chronological folds, one-session embargo, deterministic 20% ticker holdout, and model parameters were unchanged.

## Data Audit

The first immutable build was rejected before training. It contained 23,903 rows whose 24 observed bars spanned more than 120 wall-clock minutes; 11,781 of those rows were rank-eligible, or 2.32% of the eligible set. Some paths spanned as much as 360 minutes because ticker candles were missing.

Commit `27db09d` introduced label schema `ml_v3.labels.v2`. The labeler now requires a contiguous exact five-minute path through the maximum configured horizon. It drops invalid decisions and never fills, interpolates, or shifts a candle. The rejected v1 dataset was not trained.

## Decision

Reject V4-H1. It fails the predeclared requirement for positive cost-adjusted top-10 excess return in both development scopes. Do not promote B0 or R1, do not tune filters from these inspected rows, and do not open C9 shadow data for this hypothesis.
